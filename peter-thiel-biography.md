## 1. Early Life & Education

- **Full name:** Peter Andreas Thiel
- **Born:** October 11, 1967, in Frankfurt am Main, West Germany.
- **Family background:** Born to German parents. His father, **Klaus Thiel**, was a chemical engineer; his mother is **Susanne Thiel**. He has a younger brother, **Patrick Thiel**.
- **Childhood / emigration:** The family emigrated to the United States when Peter was about one year old. He spent part of his early childhood in **Cleveland, Ohio**, and later grew up in **Foster City, California** (a suburb of San Francisco).
- **Schooling:**
  - Attended **Bowditch Middle School** in Foster City.
  - Graduated from **San Mateo High School** in **1985**.
  - **Chess:** A serious junior chess player; achieved a US Chess Federation rating of ~2140 (Expert class) and was ranked among the top players in the U.S. for his age group. (See Personal Life.)
- **Stanford University:**
  - Co-founded **The Stanford Review**, a conservative/libertarian student newspaper, in **1987**, and served as its editor-in-chief.
  - Earned a **B.A. in Philosophy in 1989**.
  - Earned a **J.D. from Stanford Law School in 1992**.
- **Post-graduate:** Clerked for **Judge James Larry Edmondson** of the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Eleventh Circuit (1992–1993).

## 2. Career & Business

- **Early career:**
  - Briefly a securities lawyer at **Sullivan & Cromwell** in New York (~7 months).
  - **Derivatives trader** at **Credit Suisse** (then CS Financial Products), 1993–1996/1998.
  - Founded **Thiel Capital Management** in **1996** (his personal investment vehicle; later simply **Thiel Capital**).
- **PayPal:**
  - Co-founded **Confinity** in **1998** with **Max Levchin, Luke Nosek, and Ken Howery** (Thiel was CEO).
  - Confinity merged with **Elon Musk's X.com** in **1999/2000** to form **PayPal**.
  - After a leadership change, Thiel became **CEO of PayPal in 2000** (Musk was ousted).
  - PayPal IPO'd in **2002** and was acquired by **eBay** later that year for **~$1.5 billion**. Thiel was the largest shareholder (~$55 million personal gain).
- **Clarium Capital Management:** A global-macro hedge fund founded in **2002**. (Later wound down after losses.)
- **Palantir Technologies:** Co-founded in **2003** with **Alex Karp, Joe Lonsdale, Stephen Cohen, and Nathan Gettings**. Thiel provided seed funding; **Alex Karp** is CEO. Palantir builds data-analytics software for government and enterprise (Gotham, Foundry, AIP).
- **Founders Fund:** Venture capital firm co-founded in **2005** with **Ken Howery, Luke Nosek, and Sean Parker**. Notable investments include **SpaceX, Airbnb, Lyft, Spotify, Palantir, Anduril, Stripe**, and others.
- **Facebook (Meta):** In **August 2004**, made a **$500,000 angel investment** for a **~10.2% stake** and a board seat — Facebook's first outside investment. He remained on the board (as director) until **2022**, ultimately profiting ~$1 billion.
- **Mithril Capital Management:** Venture capital firm founded in **2012**, led by **Ajay Royan** (Thiel-backed).
- **Other notable investments / affiliations:** Early backer of **Y Combinator**, **LinkedIn**, **Asana**, **Quora**, **Spotify**, **SpaceX**, **Airbnb**, **Lyft**, **Stripe**, **Anduril**, and others. He is a prominent "angel" investor in Silicon Valley.
- **Net worth:** Estimated at roughly **$10–15 billion** (Forbes), placing him among the wealthiest people in tech.

## 3. Political Activities & Views

- **Ideology:** Self-described **libertarian**, later aligned with conservative/right-wing politics. Skeptical of democracy and globalization without technological progress.
- **Early political activity:** Supported **Ron Paul** in the 2008 and 2012 Republican presidential primaries.
- **2016 election:** Publicly **endorsed Donald Trump**, spoke at the **2016 Republican National Convention** (where he also came out publicly as gay — the first openly gay RNC speaker), and served on Trump's **transition team**. He also funded Trump's legal-defense fund.
- **2022 midterms:** Financially backed **JD Vance** (Ohio Senate, successful) and **Blake Masters** (Arizona Senate, unsuccessful). He considered but declined a **U.S. Senate run in Wyoming** in 2022.
- **2024:** Continued political engagement and support for Trump's 2024 campaign.
- **The Thiel Fellowship:** Launched in **2010** ("20 Under 20" program) — awards **$100,000** to entrepreneurs under 20 to leave or skip college and build companies/projects. Controversial for its anti-college message.
- **Seasteading:** Funded **The Seasteading Institute** in **2008** (~$500,000) to promote experimental, autonomous floating communities in international waters.
- **Views on technology & society:** Argues that technological progress has **stagnated** ("The Straussian Moment," 2004; Founders Fund manifesto "What Happened to the Future?," ~2011). Advocates "definite optimism" (from *Zero to One*). Critical of higher education and of "globalization without new technology."
- **Residence / relocation:** Long based in **San Francisco**; moved to **Los Angeles** in **2018**. Also holds **New Zealand citizenship** (granted 2011 via an investor route) and owns property near **Wanaka, New Zealand** (reportedly including a retreat/bunker).

## 4. Books & Writing

- **"The Diversity Myth: Multiculturalism and the Politics of Intolerance at Stanford" (1995)** — co-authored with **David O. Sacks**. A critique of multiculturalism and political correctness at Stanford, drawing on Thiel's experience with The Stanford Review.
- **"Zero to One: Notes on Startups, or How to Build the Future" (2014)** — co-authored with **Blake Masters**, based on Thiel's **Stanford CS183 "Startup" class (2012)**. A best-selling treatise on innovation and monopoly-building.
- **Essays / manifestos:**
  - **"The Straussian Moment" (2004)** — pamphlet/essay on politics, philosophy, and the limits of liberalism.
  - **"The Education of a Libertarian" (2009, Cato Unbound)** — outlines his libertarian, anti-democratic-leaning philosophy.
  - **"What Happened to the Future?"** — Founders Fund manifesto lamenting slowed technological progress.
- **Speeches:**
  - **2009 "The Education of a Libertarian"** essay/speech.
  - **2016 Republican National Convention speech** (Cleveland) — endorsement of Trump and public coming-out.
  - Various startup/commencement talks, including the CS183 lectures that became *Zero to One*.

## 5. Controversies & Notable Stances

- **Gawker lawsuit (Hulk Hogan / Terry Bollea v. Gawker):** Thiel **secretly funded** Hulk Hogan's privacy lawsuit against Gawker Media, which resulted in a **~$140 million judgment** and **Gawker's bankruptcy (2016)**. He revealed his role in a **2016 New York Times interview**, calling it "one of my greater philanthropic things that I've done." He has said Gawker had previously outed him as gay (2007), motivating the funding.
- **Comments on democracy / women's suffrage:** In his **2009 essay** and a **2016 interview**, he expressed skepticism about democracy and made controversial remarks suggesting the **extension of the franchise to women** and broadened suffrage had negative political consequences — widely criticized as anti-democratic and misogynistic.
- **Anti-college stance:** The Thiel Fellowship's encouragement for students to drop out has drawn criticism from educators.
- **Funding of litigation against journalists:** Beyond Gawker, his financial backing of lawsuits against media outlets has raised press-freedom concerns.
- **Association with controversial figures:** Backing of various right-wing candidates and causes has made him a polarizing figure.

## 6. Personal Life

- **Sexuality:** Openly gay; came out publicly in **2016**.
- **Spouse:** Married **Matt Danzeisen** (a finance professional) in **2017**.
- **Hobbies / interests:**
  - **Chess:** Lifelong interest; was a strong junior competitor (rated ~2140, top-age-group nationally). Has sponsored chess-related events and prizes.
  - **Longevity / life-extension:** Funds anti-aging and life-extension research (e.g., via the Thiel Foundation and support for the Methuselah Foundation / SENS).
- **Residence:** Primarily **Los Angeles, California** (since 2018); also **New Zealand** (citizen since 2011, property near Wanaka).
- **Known for:** Reserved, intellectual public persona; interest in philosophy (Hegel, Strauss), history, and contrarian thinking.
